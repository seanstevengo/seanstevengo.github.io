My life was bland - a subconscious routine.
Was I alive? Surely wasn't living.
Wasting time as an introverted teen,
The darkness I felt was unforgiving.

Minding my business when you said hello.
Startled, I froze. Felt awkwardly exposed.
My pulse getting louder, time moved so slow.
Monochrome shattered and twilight arose.

Just another girl I'd pass by, I thought.
Proved me wrong as you kept showing me more.
To light my world, your company I sought.
To show me colors I've not seen before.

And so you may never forget how you touched my life, I made this poem.
