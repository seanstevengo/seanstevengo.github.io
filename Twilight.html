twi·light
/ˈtwīˌlīt/
noun
1. the soft glowing light from the sky when the sun is below the horizon,
caused by the refraction and scattering of the sun's rays from the atmosphere.
"the twilight she gave off was sean's first glance of life"

synonyms: half-light, semidarkness, faintness, grayness, haziness
